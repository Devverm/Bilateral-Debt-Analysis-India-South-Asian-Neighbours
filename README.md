# Bilateral Debt Analysis: India & South Asian Neighbours

An analysis of India's Public & Publicly Guaranteed (PPG) bilateral lending to six South Asian neighbouring countries — Bangladesh, Bhutan, Sri Lanka, Maldives, Myanmar, and Nepal — from 2000 to 2020, based on World Bank Open Data.

![Cumulative PPG Bilateral Lending Trend](assets/lending_trend.png)

## 📊 Overview

Bilateral debt reflects the long-term external obligations that one government (or a public entity it guarantees) owes to another. This project examines two decades of PPG lending data to understand:

- How India's total bilateral lending to the region has grown over time
- Which countries receive the most (and least) lending, and how that lending has trended
- What this exposure means relative to India's overall economy and financial risk

## 🔑 Key Findings

- **90× growth**: Total PPG bilateral lending grew from under $500 Mn in 2000 to approximately **$45 Bn** by 2020.
- **Bhutan is the largest recipient**, with close to **$18 Bn** in cumulative lending — more than any other country in the dataset.
- **Nepal received the least**, at roughly **$90 Mn** over the full 20-year period.
- **2008 dip**: Lending growth briefly slowed during the Global Financial Crisis before accelerating sharply from 2010 onward.
- India's bilateral lending to its neighbours equals roughly **1.2% of India's GDP**, suggesting the country's financial exposure — even under a hypothetical default scenario — is limited.

## 🗂️ Data

- **Source**: [World Bank Open Data — International Debt Statistics (IDS)](https://data.worldbank.org/)
- **Metric**: Public & Publicly Guaranteed (PPG) external debt, bilateral lending from India
- **Coverage**: 6 countries, spanning individual country histories through 2020 (India-specific bilateral series run 2000–2020)
- **Structure**: Each per-country sheet contains `Year`, `Debtor Country` (ISO3 code), `Debt in US$`, and `YoY Growth %`
